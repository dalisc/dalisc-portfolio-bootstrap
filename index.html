NUS Data Science Challenge 2019 Runner-up

For most of us, this was our first foray into AI and data science. I thoroughly enjoyed it and this case competition heightened my interest in pursuing AI and data analytics. We were using squall data and a neural network to predict the amount of rainfall in the vicinity of a Singapore port. We used Keras and various Python libraries to engineer the features and train our neural network. Judged on our loss results and presentation, we emerged 2nd place and won a prize of S$700! You may look through our presentation slides via this link.

HackOMania 2019 Rakuten RapidAPI Challenge Winner

We were the winner of the RapidAPI Challenge at HackOMania 2019! In this hackathon, we developed and gamified a diet tracker app called Pokkapet with a twist - we incorporated the concept of a virtual pet into the app. The problem statement we dealt with was how to induce behavioural changes for diabetic patients so as to achieve lower healthcare costs. The app implemented three RapidAPI APIs and we emerged winner of the challenge! Rakuten will be publishing a blog about us soon and I will post a link here as soon as they do! The app was coded with Unity and C#. You can find the GitHub repository for it on my GitHub profile where it contains our coding. In the meanwhile, you may find a Google Slides presentation of our app via this link.

Hack&Roll 2019

Hack&Roll was the first hackathon I did with my friends. It was an exciting, really fun 24 hours of learning Python and pygame from scratch through countless websites and YouTube tutorial. We tried our hand at making a game which simulates how hard it is to get a seat on Singapore's MRT, the public train system. We envisioned a very fun game with many levels of increasing difficulty and unique non-player characters. We completed the first level we envisioned in this hackathon. It was a very enriching experience and I personally learned that software engineering is something that excites me and something that I can pick up independently via self-learning online. Our Devpost can be found here. The code to our game can be found in my GitHub profile in the repositoy named "MRTgame".

Tembusu House Photo Contest

Tembusu College is a residential college in NUS that accommodates around 600 undergraduates. The college is broken up into five houses, each of roughly 120 members. As a Tembusu Ambassador, I took charge of the annual house photo contest where each house were to submit a creative picture that represents their house while wearing the college's signature green T-shirt. The event was a success with all the houses submitting very nice and creative photos. I took charge of coordinating the manpower, liaising with house captains, liaising with judges, and distribution of prizes. While I was leading the project, I had invaluable help from my team members.

Notification Board for Weddingate

This is a notification board I designed as a UX/UI intern at Codingate in Phnom Penh, Cambodia. Aside from this, I revamped the phrasings and edited grammatical errors the English version of the Weddingate app, made proposals on how to change the layout of the app for better user experience, and helped out the marketing and sales team to brainstorm new ideas.
